Raise Amount Sanity Check

For startups seeking to raise equity investment from angels or VCs, the amount of funding you seek must make sense in relationship to your pre-money valuation. For a quick rule of thumb linking the two, first recall the basic valuation equation:

Pre-money valuation + Investment Raise Amount = Post-Money Valuation

Next, dividing your pre-money valuation by two gives you a good ballpark limit for how much you can raise in a particular funding round.

The math works out so that the investor would own 33% of the equity after the investment. For most founders, giving up a third of the company in a funding round is the high-end limit.

Savvy investors flip the equation around for a quick estimate of a reasonable pre-money valuation: two times the raise amount gives a pre-money valuation that results in a 33% ownership stake, and 3X the raise amount gives a 25% stake, and so on.
