Implied Valuation 1

Startup fundraising often involves selling equity in your startup to an investor such as an angel group or venture capital firm, trading a percentage ownership of the startup corporation for investor cash.

Funding deals start by negotiating the investment amount and pre-money valuation with investors, resulting in their ownership percentage. Alternately, investors may request a certain equity ownership percentage for their money—33% for example.

In either approach, the valuation equation determines how much ownership the startup is exchanging for the cash investment.

1. Assign a value to the startup before the investment dollars are injected. This value is called the pre-money valuation of the startup.

2. Add the investment amount to the pre- money valuation. The result is called the post-money valuation.

3. Divide the investment amount by the post-money valuation to give the equity percentage owned by the investors. This percentage is also called the founder’s dilution amount or percentage.
