True Pre-Money Valuation

VC level investment deals usually require startup founders create a stock option pool. The stock option pool is used to create additional incentives for key employees and sometimes other stakeholders such as service providers like consultants or industry experts.

The option pool is expressed as a percentage of the overall company equity, with option pool sizes of 10% to 20% being common.

The term True Pre-Money refers to the fact that the option pool creation effectively reduces the pre-money valuation negotiated between founders and investors.

To calculate the True Pre-Money valuation:

1. Multiply the option pool percentage, say 20%, by the negotiated pre-money valuation, resulting in the dollar value of the option pool.

2. Next, subtract the option pool dollar value from the negotiated pre-money valuation to give the true pre-money valuation.

Startup founders often ask investors to share in the dilution effect of the option pool creation, basing the calculation on the total post-money valuation, not the pre-money valuation.
