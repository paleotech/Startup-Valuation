Cap Table View of Valuation

A capitalization table or cap table is a document (usually an Excel spreadsheet) that tracks who owns how much of a company. Founders maintain a cap table as they grow and raise money for the startup venture, frequently referencing the cap table to help answer questions such as:

  • How much equity does each founder own?
    •  
  • How much money has been invested in the company over time?
    •  
  • Who owns what type of shares—common shares, preferred shares?
    •  
  • What are the valuation and share price at each funding stage?
    •  
  • How many stock shares have been issued to stakeholders in the venture?

    • With this calculator view, you can explore various dilution scenarios based on the two key variables:

  • The pre-money valuation you negotiate with investors
  • The amount of money you raising this investment round

    • Enter the pre-money valuation, investment amount, and the equity ownership percentages of each founder (or existing stakeholder, such as a Friends & Family investor), and review how the investment scenario plays out.

    As you can see, the higher your pre-money valuation, the less you get diluted.

    Note that the investor percentage is calculated based on the raise amount and pre-money valuation.
